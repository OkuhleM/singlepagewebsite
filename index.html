Greek mythology

Greek mythology is the body of myths originally told by the ancient Greeks,
and a genre of Ancient Greek folklore. These stories concern the origin and nature of the world,
the lives and activities of deities, heroes, and mythological creatures, and the origins and significance of the
ancient Greeks' own cult and ritual practices. Modern scholars study the myths to shed light on the religious and political institutions of ancient Greece,
and to better understand the nature of myth-making itself.

Greek Greatest Gods

the three sons of Kronos and Rhea. After a fair draw Zues became the God of the sky
Poseidon became the God of the sea and Hades became the God of the underworld.
